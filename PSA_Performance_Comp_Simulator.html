How this works. Two parts, 2026 only. (1) Fixed retainer — $5K/mo for Jul–Sep, then $8K/mo for Oct–Dec ($39K over the six-month engagement). (2) KPI success fee — a three-tier commission on full-year net revenue, gated on efficiency: nothing below the AOP target (the must-hit, covered by retainer), a trigger the moment you clear the AOP, then from AOP→stretch (Band 1), from the stretch up to 2× AOP (Band 2), and beyond 2× AOP (Band 3 accelerator). The whole fee is scaled by a blended-MER guardrail (full fee at MER ≥ , pro-rated below) so you only earn on profitable growth — not spend-bought revenue. H1 2026 (~$59.6K, banked before the engagement) counts toward the AOP line; use Actuals mode to true-up on real monthly revenue. The success fee is capped at — however far you exceed target, the variable pay stops there: a deliberate fairness ceiling so PSA's cost is bounded. Why these are realistic for an emerging skincare brand. At PSA's scale (~$230–330K revenue, one operator owning eComm + CRM + media + affiliates + influencer), a $5–8K/mo fractional retainer sits at the lean end of the beauty-DTC range ($5–15K/mo) and is far cheaper than an agency (typically 10–20% of ad spend) plus in-house hires. The success fee pays only on incremental revenue you create above an already-ambitious AOP — 10–15% of overachievement is squarely in the beauty/DTC performance-fee norm and, against a 77% gross margin with ~30% marketing, still leaves PSA the large majority of every incremental dollar. No efficiency guardrail is applied (your election); a MER floor can be added if PSA wants margin protection. Directional planning aid, not a guarantee.
